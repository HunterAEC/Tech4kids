Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about our technology learning programs, hardware requirements, or how to get involved? Find quick answers below.

What age group are the Tech4Kids modules designed for?

Our programs are structured primarily for students aged 8 to 15, with entry-level tracks for block programming and advanced paths for physical hardware builds.

Are there any costs or fees associated with the training paths?

No, all our online learning interactive resources, laboratory configurations, and video classes are completely free to public students worldwide.

How can I track student lab completion checkpoints?

Each video resource includes timestamp guides so students can systematically pause, evaluate their configuration milestones, and debug physical parts alongside our instructions.

What age group is Tech4Kids designed for?

Our content generally targets kids aged 7 to 14. We break our projects down by skill level—from "absolute beginner" blocks-based coding to "getting started with Python" for older kids.

Do I need to buy expensive software or hardware to start?

Not at all! Most of our recommended tools, programming platforms (like Scratch), and learning apps are 100% free and run right in your web browser. When we do review hardware (like a Raspberry Pi or micro:bit), we always highlight budget-friendly options.

How can parents ensure their kids are staying safe online?

Great question! Beyond installing parental controls, the best tool is communication. We recommend keeping screens in shared family spaces and checking out our Digital Citizenship guides to help kids spot safe vs. unsafe online spaces.
